Are you maximizing your school's potential?

Student’s expectations regarding their education are at an all-time high. When social media and online access allow a student to receive feedback and referrals at the tips of their fingers surrounding an educational institution, schools are under pressure to deliver an exceptional experience to maintain their reputation and ensure future enrollments. There is a heightened level of awareness amongst society when it comes to proprietary education, and every institution needs to ask themselves – Are you delivering on your promises of a quality training program?

Scotoma (“Sco-to-ma”): A partial loss of vision or a blind spot in an otherwise normal visual field. What this means – You operate your business to the best of your ability, and sometimes you try hard to improve your success rate with little to no impact on the outcome. Our service provides a bird’s eye view of areas within your school that are not reaching their potential – it brings light to the Scotomas within your organization. We then offer solutions and a sustainment plan to maintain momentum throughout your educational facility.

Who We Are

We are a consulting service that works with school owners and leadership to develop your Cosmetology school to maximize your business opportunities and ensure your students receive a world class Cosmetology education.

What We Do

We assess your business for areas of opportunity and growth both in the organizational structure of your company and developing your educational program. We form a partnership and assist in all areas of school operations to sustain profitability and excellence in instruction.

Assess

Typically a week long assessment to uncover areas of opportunity. Discussions with departments and school leadership take place during this phase.

Analyze

School ownership will be provided with a business analysis including overall report and recommendations for improvement.

Implement

A contract is established to determine the strategy of implementation in outlined areas and departments.

Sustain

Monthly touch points to ensure the organization is progressing and any necessary adjustments will be made.

Services

To stay competitive in the Cosmetology Industry, schools have to be perpetually and consistently striving towards enhancement through innovative, forward thinking. There is such potential for growth in schools all over the country, and that’s where “Business and Educational Analysis” comes in. The purpose of the consultation is to review all areas of the Beauty Academy, and assess each department of operation in order to maximize the education the students receive, and the profitability of the institution. The process will assist in producing a confident, knowledgeable, successful graduate, and heighten the reputation and financial success rate of the educational business: the goal is to create a win-win-win culture. Why settle for your current reality – Why not create and elevate the industry standard?

Included in the overall assessment of operations, you can expect to receive a review and recommendation in the following areas:

  • Curriculum review & analysis
  • Review program structure
  • Budget & expense planning
  • Guest experience
  • Assess educator knowlege & training process
  • Review the Academy's organizational structure and culture
  • Assess the institution's inventory process

About Jemma

Jemma Croney began her career working as a cosmetology educator in the Pacific Northwest. After earning several promotions she achieved a Campus Director role within a large beauty Academy.

Jemma has both her Instructor and Cosmetology license and as Campus Director she operated all aspects of the school business, including monitoring all financials affecting Academy budget and profit margins. She has written and developed curriculum for Cosmetology, Barbering, Nails and Instructor Training Programs.

Jemma has experienced success with coaching and mentoring over 50 educational staff members at a time and oversaw education for 300 students on a daily basis. Her expertise includes working closely with inventory vendors to integrate retail product lines, and kit items for staff and student use, as well as maintaining inventory levels for student kits, dispensary supplies and retail lines. She has been an intricate part of creating policies and procedures affecting staff, students and business efficiency. She believes in creating an amazing guest experience for each client that receives services at Beauty schools, and she monitored services performed on 800-1000 guests weekly.
